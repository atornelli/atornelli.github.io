Quantized Angular Momentum

A foundational understanding of quantum mechanics is called the Copenhagen Interpretation. Co-developed by Niels Bohr,a Danish physisict, this principle states that a particle exists in a superposition of possibilities until measured. The wavefunction, which stores all possible information about a particle (position, momentum, energy, etc.), is said to "collapse" once observed. Once measured, it reduces to one state.

While visiting a dear friend living in Copenhagen, I could not help but ruminate on the peculiarity of quantum mechanics. Strolling through the Design Museum Denmark, I discovered a hanging light designed by Poul Henningsen, a leading figure in Danish design, that curiously reminds me of quantized angular momentum. In this moment I felt inspired to create my own sculpture that honors my passion for quantum mechnaincs.

solder, silver wire, handmade paper, Winter 2024
